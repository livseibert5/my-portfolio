My Time in Spain

26 May, 2020

The summer after my freshman year of high school I went on a Middleburry-Monterey Language Academy immersion trip to Granada, Spain. During this month-long program, I was only allowed to speak Spanish and went from struggling to speak the language to nearly fluent by the end. It was an incredible experience to get to explore another language and culture so deeply and Spain has remained one of my favorite places to visit ever since. While I haven't been able to continue my Spanish studies in college due to engineering requirements, I have tried to keep up my skills through watching Spanish-language television such as Elite and Money Heist.

Cooking in Quarantine

24 May, 2020

Before quarantine, I was never much of a chef. However, after my online classes ended this spring I found myself with a lot of extra time on my hands and wanted to learn how to cook given that I will be living off campus this fall. At school, one of my favorite meals was ordering General Tso's Crispy Tofu from a Chinese restaurant, so I decided to try to recreate that at home. I prefer tofu over chicken because I went vegetarian 5 years ago after watching Food Inc.. The recipe turned out super well and is outlined below!

  1. Press the tofu for about 15 minutes to remove extra liquid. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees.
  2. In the mean time, mix a tablespoon of sesame oil, a tablespoon of soy sauce, a teaspoon of rice wine vinegar, half a teaspoon of garlic powder, and half a teaspoon of maple syrup in a bowl.
  3. Cut the tofu into chunks and mix it into the liquid, then mix 3 tablespoons of cornstarch in one tablespoon at a time.
  4. Put the tofu on a baking sheet and bake for 30 mins.
  5. Take the tofu out and put it in a pan over medium heat. Pour General Tso's sauce over it and let simmer for 5 mins.
